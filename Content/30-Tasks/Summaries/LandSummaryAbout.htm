Create Summary Reports

Summary reports outline landscape features and predicted worst-case fire behavior (97th percentile fuel moisture and wind). These high level landscape summaries are intended as a starting point for evaluating your area as part of the fuels management planning process.

Below, we discuss creating Summary reports from the Landscape Planning Cycle in . There is also a button that appears under selected landscapes in My Workspace labeled "Request a Report". That button only generates a summary of landscape features, not the additional Auto97th information that we discuss here from the Landscape Planning Cycle.

Navigate to Landscape Summary
  1. Go to the Planning Cycle from the top navigation menu
  2. Click the Landscape Evaluation Stage of the Cycle

  3. Click the Landscape Summary button.

 Creating a Landscape Summary Viewing a Landscape Summary

The Automatic 97th Percentile (Auto97th) fire behavior model run, and subsequent summary report, uses the capabilities of FlamMap to model fire behavior based on the 97th percentile fire weather conditions for your landscape. The summary report displays landscape file attributes, and modeled fire behavior outputs (listed below) spatially, and by percent in a bar chart, pie chart, and as a table. At the bottom right of each fire behavior and landscape feature is an Export Options button that will allow you to print the summary for that feature, or download an image (in .png format) of the pie chart, bar chart, or table.

Landscape Features Summarized:

  • Fuel Model
  • Canopy Cover (percent)
  • Stand Height (meters)
  • Canopy Base Height (meters)
  • Canopy Bulk Density (kg/m3)
  • Aspect (degrees)
  • Slope (degrees)
  • Elevation (meters)

Modeled Fire Behavior Summarized:

  • Flame Length (meters)
  • Rate of Spread (m/min)
  • Intensity (kW/m)
  • Heat per Unit Area (kJ/m2)
  • Crown Fire Activity (No Fire, Surface Fire, Passive Crown Fire, and Active Crown Fire)

Fuel Moistures and RAWS Details Summarized

Fuel moisture and RAWS details are summarized in the header of the "Model Characteristics" section of the summary report and include:

  • 97th percentile fuel moistures for 1,10,100 hr fuels; live herbaceous fuels; live woody fuels
  • Station Name
  • Station Observation Start Date
  • Station Observation End Date
  • Station Elevation
  • Station Aspect
  • Station Latitude
  • Station Longitude
Saving & Printing a Landscape Summary

Saving

In addition to being saved in My Workspace, you may also save Landscape Summary reports to your local computer. Using the download button in the top right will download the entire report as a .pdf file. Clicking the options button to the right of each section of the report, will save that specific image or graph.

Printing from your internet browser

Summary reports can also be printed directly from the internet browser. To print the full summary using Google Chrome:

  1. Right click anywhere within the white space of summary, in this example, we've clicked to the right of the summary title. This brings up several options, including Print

  2. Click Print from the options and follow your normal routine for printing a document from your computer.
