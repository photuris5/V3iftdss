Create Summary Reports

The first step in working with landscapes in is to create a Landscape File (.lcp file) and evaluate the landscape and potential modeled fire behavior. Here you can create a landscape file and requests an automated report to summarize the vegetation characteristics and modeled fire behavior outputs for your specific landscape based on approximated worst case (97th percentile fuel moisture and wind) conditions. These high level landscape summaries are intended as a starting point for to further evaluate your area in the fuels management planning purposes.

Basic Functions

In the Generate Landscape Summary task you can:

  1. Specify an existing landscape for a summary
  2. Specify an area of interest (polygon or shapefile) within an existing landscape for a summary
  3. View the landscape or area of interest in (Requires navigating back to this task when finished)
  4. Create a summary report for the landscape features and fire behavior modeled under 97th percentile weather conditions
  5. Create a new Landscape from within the Landscape Generation and Summary task
  6. Create a Landscape from within
Generating the Landscape Summary Viewing a Landscape Summary

The Automatic 97th Percentile (Auto97th) fire behavior model run, and subsequent summary report, uses the capabilities of FlamMap to model fire behavior based on the 97th percentile fire weather conditions for your landscape. The summary report displays landscape file attributes, and modeled fire behavior outputs (listed below) spatially, and by percent in a bar chart, pie chart, and as a table. At the bottom right of each fire behavior and landscape feature is an Export Options button that will allow you to print the summary for that feature, or download an image (in .png format) of the pie chart, bar chart, or table.

Landscape Features Summarized:

  • Fuel Model
  • Canopy Cover (percent)
  • Stand Height (meters)
  • Canopy Base Height (meters)
  • Canopy Bulk Density (kg/m3)
  • Aspect (degrees)
  • Slope (degrees)
  • Elevation (meters)

Modeled Fire Behavior Summarized:

  • Flame Length (meters)
  • Rate of Spread (m/min)
  • Intensity (kW/m)
  • Heat per Unit Area (kJ/m2)
  • Crown Fire Activity (No Fire, Surface Fire, Passive Crown Fire, and Active Crown Fire)

RAWS Details Summarized

RAWS details are summarized in the header of the 'Model Characteristics' section of the summary report and include:

  • Station Name
  • Station Observation Start Date
  • Station Observation End Date
  • Station Elevation
  • Station Aspect
  • Station Latitude
  • Station Longitude
Printing a Landscape Summary

In addition to viewing and downloading landscape summaries, they may also be printed from your browser. To print the full summary using Google Chrome:

  1. Select the portion of the summary you wish to print (from the Report Characteristics panel on the left of the summary).
  2. Right click anywhere within the white space of summary, in this example, we've clicked to the right of the summary title. This brings up several options, including Print
  3. Click Print from the options and follow your normal routine for printing a document from your computer.
