Landscape Fire Behavior (LFB) Outputs

Fire behavior outputs are displayed spatially and include: 

Name Units Brief Description
Crown Fire Activity

Fire Classes:
No, Surface, passive crown, active crown

 A surface fire may make the transition to some form of crown fire depending on surface fire intensity and crown characteristics. The crown characteristics used to compute crown fire activity include canopy base height, stand height, canopy bulk density, and foliar moisture content.
Direction of Maximum Spread Degrees The heading direction of a fire. Direction of maximum spread results from the algebraic combination of the wind vector and slope vector.
Fireline Intensity BTU/ft-sec The rate of energy or heat release per unit length of fire front, regardless of its depth. In other words, the energy released at the flaming front of an actively burning fire.
Flame Length Feet The distance from the ground at the leading edge of the flame to the tip of the flame.
Heat per Unit Area BTU per foot2 Total amount of heat released per unit area as the flaming front of the fire passes.
Rate of Spread Chains/Hour The linear rate of advance of a fire front in the direction perpendicular to the fire front.
