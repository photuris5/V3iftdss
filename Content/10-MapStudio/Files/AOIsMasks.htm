Using Shapes or Shapefiles in for Areas of Interest and Landscape Masks

In you will encounter several terms that are applicable to shapefiles (areas of interest, landscape masks, polygons). These terms all describe shapefiles, but are worded differently in different tasks to reflect the terminology common to those tasks in the wildland fire community. Here is a brief overview of how these terms are used in .

Area of Interest and Landscape Mask

Area of Interest indicates that you can select a shapefile to constrain your modeling or analysis to a specific area, such as a treatment unit. This term is used in the Landscape Summary task and Determining Treatment Alternatives task.

In the Editing Landscape task, you will also see the term Landscape Mask used. Landscape Mask will constrain your edits to a specific area.

While we use different terms, Area of Interest and Landscape Mask function in the same way. Consider for example a scenario with a landscape called ‘Shady Basin’ and a treatment unit designated by a shapefile ‘Unit A’. In Editing, you would select Shady Basin as your landscape, and use Landscape Mask drop-down menu to constrain your edits to ‘Unit A’. In modeling fire behavior, you would select your edited landscape, and use the Area of Interest drop-down menu to constrain your modeling to Unit A.

Shapefile, Polygon, and Mask

Shapefile, Shape, Polygon, and Mask are used in Map Studio to refer to shapefiles.

  • Shapes:Masks: is used in the Layer List widget to indicate shapefiles.
  • Shapefile: Is used in the Shapefile Upload widget.
  • Polygons: Is used in the Create/Edit Shapes widget.
