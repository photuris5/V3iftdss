Fuels Treatment Effectiveness Monitoring (FTEM) Database access in .

The Fuels Treatment Effectiveness Monitoring (FTEM) database is an interagency database designed to demonstrate the utility of fuels treatments by documenting their effects on wildfire. Within FTEM you may view existing, and report new, fire and fuel treatment interactions. provides an interface by which you may review information from, or contribute information to FTEM, however the FTEM database itself is stored outside of the application.

FTEM Background and Technical Documentation

The Fuels Treatment Effectiveness Monitoring (FTEM) database meets the following objectives:

  • Document when hazardous fuel treatments are encountered by wildfire and to what degree they are effective
  • Establish baseline data to assist with making future hazardous fuels program decisions
  • Provide documentation to meet land management goals while utilizing a minimum of information
  • Provide the capability to record additional information such as narratives, reports, maps, and imagery that can be used to document lessoned learned and adjust future treatment designs and prescriptions
