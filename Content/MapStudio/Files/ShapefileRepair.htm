How to Repair Uploaded Shapefiles

When uploading shapefiles with multiple adjoining polygons, will automatically join the polygons into a single dataset; this process may create erroneous gaps or vertices where these polygons once touched. The steps below outline the process of repairing or cleaning these polygons and re-saving them as a new shapefile.

These occasional gaps in adjoining polygons are a known issue which we will be resolving in a forthcoming version of .

Step 1 Viewing your shapefile in

Once you have uploaded and saved your shapefile, navigate to the Layer List, a checked box to the left of the shapefile confirms your layer is operational. If this box is not checked, click on the box to select it. If your shapefile is not visible in the Layer List, you may have to add it to your map. Zoom to your shapefile by selecting the Zoom To option under More Options in the top right of the Layer List .

Step 3 Edit your shapefile

You can also fill holes in shapefiles at this point in the process. Simply click the extent polygon or freehand buttons within the Edit Tool and draw a small polygon over the hole you wish to fill. Upon saving your edits, the smaller polygon will be merged with the larger one, effectively patching the hole.
