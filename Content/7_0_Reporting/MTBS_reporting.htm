Monitoring Trends in Burn Severity (MTBS) Reporting

 

MTBS Background

The Monitoring Trends in Burn Severity (MTBS) project generates burn severity data, maps, and reports to evaluate trends in burn severity at the local, state, and national levels to help develop and assess the effectiveness of land management decisions. This information provides a baseline from which to monitor the recovery and health of ﬁre-affected landscapes over time. Spatial and tabular data quantifying burn severity is available to estimate risk associated with a range of current and future resource threats.

MTBS uses Landsat data and the difference Normalized Burn Ratio (dNBR) algorithm to map burn severity of all ﬁres since 1984 greater than 500 acres in the east, and 1000 acres in the west.
