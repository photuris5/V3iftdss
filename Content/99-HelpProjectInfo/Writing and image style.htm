Writing and image style

In the help you will see content written in second person, for a casual tone.

Most images are in line, or floated right, depending on what appears better on the page.

Images typically have labels, shapes or arrows, in red, and 1 px black border (added in Snagit)

You'll notice many drop-downs throughout the project

drop-downs have been used in many areas where heading2 would have been used in the IFTDSS 2.0 Help, to offer a lot of material on one page, but in a way that can be summarized at a glance. This was a handy approach for the 'about' pages. Help pages whose file names end in 'about' and are linked to the IFTDSS application. They appear short initially, you can expand the drop-downs to view additional content as needed.

Notification styles for divs:

text marked 'important' indicates information you need to know for the run or operation to successfully complete .

a note includes helpful information that applies to the task you are performing.

a tip includes information that will save time, or make subsequent processes in IFTDSS easier

text here describes features that will be added to in the near future

Referring to buttons and parts of

Specific buttons and Parts of the interface are given the span.UIComponent style

i.e. to upload a shapefile use the Shapefile Upload tool.

Sometimes were refer to parts of that aren't buttons, but parts of the application. These typically indicated by capitalization

i.e. To get started, go to the Landscape Evaluation stage of the Planning Cycle.

Notes to Ourselves

Sometimes its helpful to add details for content that we, or other groups, will add later. These are given the '.comment' style, and assigned the 'Developer Notes' condition (so only we'll see them).
