Adding Shapefiles in FTEM

For larger point wildfires with no perimeter (polygon) showing in FTEM, you may find it helpful to upload and view a polygon yourself using GeoMac.gov or your own file, and the Upload Shape widget on the FTEM map. You can tell there is no polygon coming into FTEM by the Wildfire point symbol.

Note that this will not save your shapefile, it is intended to be viewed while working with wildfires in treatments in the FTEM system. To permanently add wildfires and treatments to FTEM, please contact your Agency Lead, their contact information is displayed on the FTEM mainpage.

To add a shapefile from your local computer
  1. Click the Add Shapefile widget.

  2. Click Upload File.

    The FTEM Add Shapefile feature adds a shapefile to the map interface from your local computer. To add your shapefile, it must be compressed as a .zip file, and contain at least shp, .shx, .dbf, and .prj files.

  3. Search your local computer file directory, select it, and click Open.

  4. Check any fields to include.

  5. Click Add to Map.

  6. Your shapefile is added to the map and you are automatically zoomed to its extent.

 To obtain a shapefile from GeoMac and add it

  1. Note your fire name, date, and state in FTEM.

  2. Open a new browser window and navigate to https://www.geomac.gov/

  3. Select the Download Data option from GeoMac's website (currently found in the left-hand menu)

  4. In the file list that opens, narrow your search by date, state, and fire, then scroll down to the bottom of the list until you see a .zip file listed

  5. Download the zip file to your local machine.
  6. Use “Upload Shapefile” widget from within FTEM, described earlier on this topic page.
