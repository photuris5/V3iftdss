Using FTEM

The FTEM interface is arranged to walk you through the process. Use the tabs to select a wildfire, select your treatment areas, enter monitoring data, and review and submit your data. Below, we walk through each stage of this process. The follow steps assume you have an FTEM account (If not, visit the FTEM Setup Topic for instructions). Upon opening FTEM, you will see wildfires with a split view of on the left, and a table view on the right.

Wildfire:

Pick a wildfire based on year and wildfire name:

  1. Enter a Wildfire Year, the year the wildfire was declared under control, or extinguished.
  2. (Optional) Filter your results by agency.
  3. (Optional) Use the wildfire search field to further constrain your results by entering the fire name.
  4. Select the desired fire name from the list by left clicking.

    5. Need to un-select your fire? Hover over the green checkmark next to the fire name in the tabs along the top. When the checkmark turns into a red x, click the x to unselect your fire.

  5. Optional: Click the Zoom To button to zoom to the fire on the map, or the Table Summary button to see a summary of the interactions for that fire.
  6. Proceed to the Treatments Tab. You may also proceed to Treatments by clicking the Select Treatments button located to the left of the Zoom To button.

Notice that each wildfire listed is followed by information, left to right, describing the number of acres, control date, jurisdiction unit, and the number of interactions.

 Treatments: Select your treatments

If a treatment is within you're agencies jurisdiction you will see an empty check box to the left.

Select only the treatments that interacted with the wildfire.

  1. (Optional) If you know there are treatments that interacted with the wildfire, but do not see them here, click the Add Treatment(s) button to search and select treatments near the fire (This option will allow you to choose a buffer distance to search within).
  2. If there are treatments that should not be on this list, or did not interact with the fire, use the checkbox to select them and click the Remove Treatment(s) button.
  3. Once you have all desired treatments, select them either individually by clicking the check box to the left of the treatment name(s), or by using the Select All button.
    Note the colored icons to the left of the treatment names. In this example the clouds indicate these treatments are polygons, and their red color indicates monitoring/data entry for them has not been started. Learn more about these symbols by visiting the FTEM Symbology Topic.
  4. Proceed to Monitor Tab. You may also get to Monitoring by clicking the Monitor Treatment(s) button at the bottom right of the list.

Notice that each treatment listed is followed by information, left to right, describing the treatment ID, type of treatment, date of completion, and agency. number of acres, control date, jurisdiction unit, and the number of interactions.

 Monitor: Enter your data

  1. Select the treatments to enter data for by clicking the checkbox next each treatment(s).
  2. Click the Enter Data button.
  3. A series of questions for the treatment(s) interaction will appear. Notice that each blue heading expands to list of specific questions. The first header, Interaction Details and Treatment Effects, is required. Enter data into each field. We briefly describe guidance for data in each field in the FTEM Required Questions Guidance Topic.
  4. Proceed to any optional fields and enter data. We briefly describe guidance for data in each field in the FTEM Optional Questions Guidance Topic.
  5. Once your data is entered, click Save and Close to proceed. If you want to save your partially completed work and resume the rest of the process later, click Save.
    6. Note that in this example two of three treatments are selected for data entry. You can choose to enter the same data for all your treatments, or like this example, enter data for some treatments, and later enter different data for other treatments. This can be useful if multiple treatments were affected, but burned under different conditions.
  6. Proceed to the review tab.
 Review: Review and submit your data

In the Review tab, use the Table Summary button to generate a review table of treatments and data entered. At this point, if you are satisfied with your treatment data, you may proceed to step B. However, if you find a data entry that needs editing or additional information, you can use the tabs to navigate back, select treatments, and amend data.

Use the Complete Wildfire Monitoring button to submit your data to the FTEM System.

At the bottom of the tab, the number of completed, in progress, and un-started treatments is displayed.

Note the Complete Wildfire Monitoring will be grayed out until all treatments are completed.
