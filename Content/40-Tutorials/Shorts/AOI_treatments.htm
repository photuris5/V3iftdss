Creating Treatment Polygons

To simulate and evaluate the impact of treatments, we'll delineate our planned treatment areas with shapes in . First we will create a polygon for each treatment area, next we will combine those into one shapefile so we can use the combined file in our comparison.

First, we'll create three individual shapes:

  1. Open Create/Edit Shapes
  2. Select your method, in this case the freehand draw method
  3. Draw your polygon. You can use the delete button to remove the polygon if you are not satisfied with it.
  4. Give your polygon a name, such as "Treatment Unit 1"
  5. Specify a folder to store the file, in this case "Haypress Creek Project"

  6. Click Create. Now repeat the process and draw two additional polygons. You may refer to the image at the bottom of this page for a visual reference if you prefer to approximate the polygons we drew in this example. Your polygons do not need to exactly match our example.

Your polygons will initially show up highlighted in yellow. You will want to save the three of these as one shape for later use in . To do this:
