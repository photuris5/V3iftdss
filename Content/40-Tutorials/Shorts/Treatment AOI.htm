Creating Treatment Polygons

To simulate and evaluate the impact of treatments, we'll delineate our planned treatment areas with shapes in . First by creating a polygon for each treatment area, then by combining those into one shapeto be used in a landscape comparison later.

To Create a Shape:

  1. Open Create/Edit Shapes
  2. Select your method, in this case the freehand draw method
  3. Draw your first polygon.

    4. You can use the delete button to remove the polygon if you are not satisfied with it. In this example, draw three polygons.

  4. Give the polygon a name, in this case "Treatment Unit 1"
  5. Specify a folder to save the polygon in: "Haypress Creek Project"

  6. Click Create

After your first polygon is complete, repeat this process for your second and third polygons. For this example they should approximate the image below, but they do not need to be exact.

Now, save all three individual shapes as one:

When completed, your polygons will change from highlighted to a purple shaded outline on the map as shown below.

Saved shapes are available in My Workspace in the folder you selected (in this case the Haypress Creek Project folder).
