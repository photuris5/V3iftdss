Comparing Landscapes to Develop Treatment Alternatives

Develop alternatives to determine how different types of fuels treatments and their spatial arrangement on the landscape affect landscape characteristics and resultant fire behavior. You may generate maps and reports to compare landscapes and fire behavior model outputs, and use outcomes to determine the best fuels management strategy to meet your project objectives. In this task you will:

  1. Select or create an un-edited (originating) landscape
  2. Edit landscape characteristics to create alternative(s)
  3. Enter fire behavior model inputs
  4. Run fire behavior models on alternative(s)
  5. Compare landscape characteristic alternatives and resultant fire behavior outputs on maps and in reports

All edited landscapes, resultant model run outputs, and comparison reports created in this workflow will be filed in your workspace in the same location as landscape you choose in the following step as your originating landscape.

To view both your landscape, and editing rules at the same time, use the View Split Map button at the top of the page.

Choose an Originating Landscape

Use this tab to create or select a starting unedited landscape. The landscape selected in this step initiates your comparison session and will be marked with “0” throughout the workflow. The “0” indicates the original landscape extracted from LANDFIRE that has not been edited. It will be available for you to edit in the Edit Landscapetab. If landscape “0” does not represent your Existing Condition (EC) you can edit it to create your existing condition in the Edit Landscape tab.

The landscape you use to develop and compare treatment alternatives should mimic, as closely as possible, conditions as they exist on the ground, the Existing Condition (EC). When you select your landscape "0", any landscapes that match that extent will become available in the subsequent steps of the Landscape Comparison process. Thus, if you had edited a landscape previously to match the existing condition, choose the original un-edited file, and the edited version will be available in the next steps of Landscape Comparison.

If your landscape “0” does represent your EC, you can begin editing to create your treatment alternatives.

To complete this step either:

Use the Select Landscape drop-down menu to select an existing un-edited landscape from your workspace or,

Use the Create on Map option to draw a new landscape extent and extract a new Landfire landscape

 Edit Your Landscape

Here you will edit landscapes to develop your Existing Condition (EC) if needed, and your treatment alternatives. The only landscape(s) available to edit at this point will be unedited LANDFIRE landscapes (these will be referred to as the “0” landscape) and any landscapes matching that extent. Create your treatment alternatives by altering your EC landscape, whether that’s the "0" landscape, an existing landscape that has been made available in this step, or one that you create in this editing step from landscape "0".

It is very important to have a landscape that represents EC to ensure more accurate comparison results in gauging how each alternative does or does not meet objectives. The EC may need to reflect new disturbances on your landscape, like recent wildfires, fuels treatments, or windfalls.

Once you have established your EC, you can use that landscape as your originating landscape and create as many treatment alternatives as necessary to make your comparisons. Steps include:

  1. Select a originating landscape in the Originating Landscape drop down box
  2. Use Default Edit Rules or Landscape Edit Rules and Landscape Masks (optional) to edit your landscape
  3. Give your edited landscape a unique name in the Edited Landscape box at the bottom of the page.
  4. Click Save New Landscape
  5. Use the refresh button in the Originating Landscape drop down to check to see if your new landscape is available to view
  6. Once your edited landscape has been created, you can choose it in the Originating Landscape drop down to view for accuracy and/or to edit and create a new landscape to compare. Use the View Split Map button to view the map output with your edits, if you have not used this setting already.

Continue this process to create as many landscapes as needed to compare. When you are finished editing and creating new landscapes, continue on to the Model Input tab to establish conditions under which the landscapes you just created will be modeled.

The order of your landscape rules is very important! Details on performing landscape edits are available in the Landscape Editing Topic.

It is important that the landscape you use to develop and compare treatment alternatives mimics, as closely as possible, conditions as they exist on the ground.

Use the extend/retract arrows to either view only the input page, view both the input page and map studio in split screen , or view only the map in the map studio.

 Establish Model Inputs

Use this tab to establish the model state by which all landscapes in your comparison session will be run and compared. If you are unsure as to what your model state should be for your comparison session, utilize My Modeling Playground to test different weather and fuel moisture conditions on your landscape(s). Creating an Auto97th Landscape Fire Behavior report in the Landscape Summary task of the Landscape Evaluation stage of the planning cycle. can also help give you a starting point for establishing model inputs.

Once a model is run in Landscape Comparison, it is permanent, there is no way to re-run that model. For this reason, testing out various modeling inputs in MyModeling Playground before proceeding with the rest of Landscape Comparison is recommended.

To complete this step:

  1. Type wind speed and wind direction in the Wind section
  2. Select Crown Fire Method and enter Foliar Moisture Content in the Crown Fire Inputs section
  3. Populate the timelag and herbaceous and woody fuel moisture percentages in the Initial Fuel Moisture section (Optional)
  4. Add rows to establish fuel moistures for individual fuel models
  5. Save Inputs

For full details about the fire behavior modeling, inputs, and outputs, visit the Fire Behavior Basic Topic.

You can adjust model inputs even after they have been saved during your session as long as you have not run a model.

Once you have run a model in the Run Model tab with saved inputs, they cannot be modified in this session.

If you are unsure what you should use for model inputs, utilize the IFTDSS Playground to test out different weather and fuel moisture condition scenarios and/or the Auto97th Landscape Fire Behavior run and reports in the Landscape Evaluation>Landscape Summary portion of the Cycle.

 Run Your Model

The Run Model tab provides a space where you can re-name and run models for landscapes you created in the Edit Landscape tab. The Landscape column in the Run Model table will list landscape “0” as selected in the Pick Landscape tab and all of its derivatives. Again, as mentioned in previous tabs, landscapes edited off of landscape “0” outside of this workflow will be available here as well. You can model and compare those landscapes just the same as if you created them within this session. The Run Name box will be auto populated with the landscape name and the type of model being run.

Currently only runs Landscape Fire Behavior but more models will be integrated soon.

Feel free to accept the default name or rename your model run. Now you can produce Landscape Fire Behavior for landscapes of your choosing by selecting Run Model after the landscapes you would like to compare. Use the refresh button to check status.

To complete this step:

  1. Click the Refresh button next to Landscape in the Run Models table to be sure all of your landscapes are listed
  2. (Optional) Rename model runs for each landscape
  3. (Optional)Revisit Model Input tab to be sure your inputs are as you would like themOnce you have run a model in the Run Model tab, the inputs are locked in for this landscape in the Treatment Comparison workflow. Meaning you will not be able to select landscape “0” and rerun it or any of its derived landscapes with alternative inputs in the Develop Treatment Alternatives workflow. This is to ensure model outputs that are being compared are commensurate. We are working on a Modify Inputs option to alleviate this inconvenience.
  4. Click Run Model for landscapes you would like to compare
  5. Click the Refresh button next to Run Status in the Run Models table to check the status of your model outputs
Compare Alternatives

The Compare Alternatives tab lists Landscape "0" and all the landscapes match that landscape extent, and all the landscapes that have been created by editing (landscape alternatives, an Existing Condition landscape, etc.). Model runs created and completed in the Run Model tab will also be listed here.

  • Use the Refresh button next to Landscape in the table to ensure all landscapes and respective model runs are listed
  • Click on individual landscape or model run names to open the split screen map and view.
  • You can use the checkboxes next to a row to select and then View on Map to view that particular landscape and its outputs

To Evaluate Comparisons:

  • Select the checkbox for two landscapes and model runs you would like to compare
  • The first box you check will be tagged with “1” and should represent your “treated” landscape.
  • The second box you check will be tagged with “2” and should represent landscape/model run you want the first landscape/model run to be compared against, or EC.

Example: My objective is to reduce flame length on my treated landscape. So, I need to show change due to treatment. I need my output grid to show that flame length was reduced by treatment. So, AltA(FL 2)-EC(FL 10)= FL -8. I reduced my FL by 8. Grid will show -8 meaning an 8 foot reduction.

Once you have checked 2 boxes you can:

  • View on Map where, if it is not already open, will open the split screen and will display the difference grid for the landscapes you choose. The layers list should also be open and will list the two landscapes, two model outputs, and two difference grids (landscape diff and model output diff) to view.
  • Compare in Summary Report where a pop up box will come up for you to select Create Report for either or both of Landscape Compare or Fire Behavior Compare. Use the refresh button to check status. Once the report has been created you can click on the blue hyper link opening a new tab where you can view, download or print your report.
