Using FTEM

The FTEM interface walks you through the treatment monitoring process. Use the tabs to select a wildfire, select your treatments, enter monitoring data, and review and complete your monitoring. Below, we walk through each stage of this process. The following steps assume you have an FTEM account. If not, visit the FTEM Setup Topic for instructions. Upon opening FTEM, you will see wildfires with a split view of on the left, and a table view on the right.

  • Jurisdictional Agencies Reference Layer

 Step 1: Pick Your Wildfire

Pick a wildfire based on year and wildfire name:

  1. Enter a Wildfire Year, the year the wildfire was declared under control, or extinguished.
  2. (Optional) If you are affiliated with multiple agencies, filter your results by agency. If you are not an administrator or dual agency editor, this option may not be visible.
  3. (Optional) Use the wildfire search field to further constrain your results by entering the fire name. Note the search field turns yellow when you begin typing, and may be closed by clicking the red magnifying glass icon.
  4. Click the necessary wildfire name on the list to select it. This auto-populates the next tab, Treatments, with a list of all treatments that overlapped selected fire. See the Buffering Rules documentation for more information on how treatments are auto-populated.

    5. Need to de-select your fire? Move the mouse cursor to the Wildfire tab and click the black X to the left of the wildfire name.

  5. (Optional) Click the Zoom To button to zoom to the fire on the map, or the Table Summary button to see a summary of the interactions for that fire.

  6. Proceed to the Treatments tab by clicking the tab. You may also proceed to Treatments by clicking theTreatments button located under the wildfire name you've selected.

Attached files can be viewed, added, or downloaded at anytime if needed (see the Attachments topic). Notice that each wildfire listed is followed by information, left to right, describing the number of acres, control date, monitor status, and the number of interactions.

 Step 2: Select Treatments

In this step you will choose all the treatments for your agency that you know interacted or influenced the fire to form a list of interacting treatments. These treatments will be monitoring in the next tab (Monitoring). You can think of this treatment selection step as the "shopping cart" where you place the treatments that require monitoring, and remove the ones that don't.

The Treatment list for a fire is shared by all agency personnel monitoring the fire, you may need to collaborate with monitors from adjacent units to make sure you do not remove treatments others will need to monitor.

  1. If you know there are treatments that interacted with the wildfire, but do not see them here, use the Add Treatment(s) button to search and select treatments. This button allows you to search a list of all treatments within a specific buffer distance from the fire.
  2. If there are treatments that did not interact with the fire, use the check box to select them and use the Remove Treatment(s) button. Removed treatments may be added back to the list using the Add Treatments(s) button if needed.
  3. Once all the necessary treatments are listed, select them using the checkboxes, or using the Select All button.

    Note the colored icons to the left of the treatment names. In this example the polygon icons indicate these treatments are polygons, and their red color indicates monitoring/data entry for them has not been started. Learn more about these symbols by visiting the FTEM Symbology Topic.

  4. Proceed to Monitor by clicking the Monitor tab, or by clicking one of the Monitor Treatment(s) buttons at the top or bottom of the treatment list.

Notice that each treatment listed is followed by information describing the treatment ID, type of treatment, date of completion, number of acres, and agency.

 Step 3: Enter Monitoring Data
  1. Select a treatment to monitor using the checkbox next to it.

  2. Click the Enter Data button to start monitoring the treatment.

    By using the check box for one or more treatment, you may choose to enter data for treatments individually, or in batches. Note that when running batches, some fields will still need to be revisited and filled individually for each treatment such as time of burn and acres burned.

  3. A series of questions for the treatment(s) interaction appears as a series of drop-down headings addressing specific monitoring questions. The first header, "Interaction Details and Treatment Effects" is required. Enter data into each field. Guidance for monitoring each required field is described in the FTEM Required Questions Guidance Topic.
  4. Proceed to any optional fields and enter data. Guidance for monitoring each optional field is described in the FTEM Optional Questions Guidance Topic.
  5. Once your data is entered, click Save and Close to proceed. If you want to save your partially completed work and resume the rest of the process later, click Save. You may also choose to Close and Discard Changes.

    6. Note that in the image below two of three treatments are selected for data entry. You can choose to "batch" enter the same data for a selected set of treatments, but some fields such as time and acreage will still need to be revisited and filled individually. You may find this approach useful if multiple treatments were involved in the same interaction.

  7. Proceed to the Complete tab, to finalize your monitoring.

    Note that even when monitored treatments appear completed, indicated by a green point or polygon symbol, the wildfire will not be completed until are interactions for a given agency are monitored and submitted using he Complete Wildfire Monitoring button in the Complete tab.

Step 4: Complete the Process by Reviewing and Submitting your Data

In the Complete tab:

  1. Click the Table Summary button to generate a review table of treatments and data entered. You may also review attachments using the Attachments button. At this point, if you are satisfied with your treatment data, you may proceed to Step B. However, if you find a data entry that needs editing or additional information, you can use the tabs to navigate back, select treatments, and modify data.

    A list of "completed", "in progress", and "not started" treatments is displayed at the bottom of the page for your agency. If multiple agencies are involved, this will be displayed for each agency.

  2. Click the Complete Wildfire Monitoring button to submit your data to the FTEM System. The monitoring will be available in report queries from FTEM reporting.

     

If the Complete Wildfire Monitoring button is grayed out, there are still treatments unmonitored for your agency. To make the button active, you will either need to finish monitoring treatments for your agency in the monitoring tab, remove treatments that did not interact with the fire in the Treatments tab, or collaborate with personnel in your agency on adjacent units who still need to monitor their treatments.
