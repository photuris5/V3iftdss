Landscape Summary Report

A landscape summary report provides a quick way to run a fire behavior model under severe weather conditions for a landscape and view results both geospatially, and as a report. Fuel moistures are automatically populated using the 97th percentile weather conditions recorded at the Remote Automated Weather Station (RAWS) nearest to your area. (More information on Auto97th RAWS selection can be found in the Landscape/Auto97th Summary Report Documentation Topic).

Following along from the previous tutorial, we'll begin these instructions from the Landscape Summary page. If you had navigated away from that page: You can return to it via the Cycle option in the top navigation menu, click the Landscape Evaluation stage of the cycle, and click the Landscape Summary option.

To generate a Landscape Summary from the Landscape Summary page:

Make sure your landscape of choice appears in the Select Landscape menu, in this example, "HaypressLF2012unedited". Leave the Area of Interest blank for this example, but if you had one to constrain your summary area, it could be entered here. Click the Request a Report button.

  1. Click Create Report when the prompt appears with your landscape name, that will generate the Summary Report.

  2. The icon indicates the Summary Report is being generated. You can use the Refresh button to check the status of the report. It may take a couple minutes to generate.

  3. When finished, your Summary Report will become an active hyperlink, and a green checkmark will appear next to it. Clicking it from this confirmation dialogue box will open the report. Later, as you navigate through IFTDSS, you will be able to view this report again from My Workspace, in the same folder as your landscape.

  4. Click the Report link and give it a quick overview to become familiar with its layout. Note the landscape data and model run parameters outlined along the top, the map, table, bar, and pie charts available for the landscape and behavior outputs, and the ability to jump to a specific output using the left-hand menu. When finished, click the download icon in the top-right, and download a pdf to your location machine.

  5. With your summary is created, navigate to My Workspace, and view the fire behavior outputs in Map Studio:

    1. Click on My Workspace
    2. Click the Haypress Creek Project folder
    3. Find the Landscape summary model output "HaypressCrkLF2012unedited-Auto97th".

    4. Click the View on Map button.

  6. Use the Layer List and Zoom to evaluate fire behavior across your landscape, focusing in on theSierra City wildand urban interface in the northeast of the map.

    1. Open the Basemap Gallery
    2. Select "Imagery with Labels"
    3. Open Layer List
    4. Make sure your model output is checked and note the model outputs available (flame length, rate of spread, fireline intensity, heat per unit area, crown fire activity, and maximum spread direction).
    5. You may need to adjust the transparency to better view the outputs with your map imagery. Click the More Options button to the right of the "Model Outputs" layer, and click Transparency. A slider will allow you to adjust the layer transparency. The model layer in this imagery has a transparency of about 60%.

    6. Zoom into the Sierra City area by double left-clicking, or using the + Zoom widget in the top left of the map.

    7. As you evaluate your landscape you may find it helpful to adjust the layer transparency using the more options button to the right of your fire behavior layer, and the different basemap galleryoptions.
